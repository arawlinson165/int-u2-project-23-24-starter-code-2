Ohio River Pollution


The Ohio River supplies all of Evansville’s water, yet it is extremely polluted. Find out more about what causes this pollution, where it is, and what you can do to help below.

Causes of Pollution

Agricultural runoff, industrial waste discharge, outdated sewers, and fracking all cause pollution (“Water Pollution”). In Evansville, superfund sites, areas of elevated pollution, contaminate both soil and water. The Jacobsville Superfund Site, for example, has large amounts of lead and arsenic in the soil from industrial activity through the 1950s and extends to the river (“Jacobsville”).

Pigeon Creek

107 acres of land drain into Pigeon Creek as part of the Lower Pigeon Creek Watershed (“Lower”). Agricultural runoff pollutes it with nitrates, E-coli, excess sediment, and phosphorus. Flow is also reduced by logjams and trash dumped in the creek (“Little”).

Get Involved

You can volunteer for the Warrick County SWCD, Evansville Water and Sewer Utility, and Keep Evansville Beautiful for water pollution reduction and general trash cleanup events. ORSANCO also helps organize river sweeps, which sometimes happen in Evansville, and you can even host one on your own! Falls of the Ohio State Park and other parks also have events dedicated to cleaning up the river.
